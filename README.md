# Healthcare-Data-Analysis-and-Insights
This project analyzes a healthcare dataset including medical examination, hospitalization details, and patient profiles to uncover insights on health conditions, histories, and costs. Using data cleaning, transformation, and analysis, it identifies trends and relationships to support data-driven decisions.

---
📌 Project Overview
---
This project focuses on analyzing healthcare data by integrating customer profiles, medical examination records, and hospitalization details into a unified dataset. The objective is to explore patient health characteristics, identify medical risk patterns, and understand factors influencing healthcare charges through data analysis and visualization.

Using Microsoft Excel, healthcare data from multiple tables was combined and analyzed to derive meaningful insights that can support healthcare providers, insurers, and policymakers in making informed decisions.

---
🎯 Problem Statement
---
Healthcare organizations manage large volumes of patient information across multiple systems. Analyzing these datasets can help uncover trends in patient health conditions, treatment history, and healthcare expenses.

---
🔍 Objectives
---
- Merge healthcare-related datasets using a common customer identifier.
- Analyze health indicators such as BMI, HbA1C, cancer history, smoking habits, transplant history, and diabetes status.
- Investigate factors affecting healthcare charges.
- Visualize trends and relationships between demographic and medical variables.
- Generate actionable insights through data-driven analysis.

---
📂 Dataset Integration
---
A new worksheet named "Healthcare" was created by combining three source tables using Customer ID as the common key.

Data Integration Method
---
- Used VLOOKUP to merge records from multiple tables.
- Ensured consistency and accuracy across all datasets.
- Removed redundant columns and retained only relevant attributes.

💡 Key Insights
---
- Obesity is the strongest contributor to healthcare expenses in the dataset.
- Healthcare costs increase significantly with age, particularly among older adults.
- HbA1C levels tend to rise with age, indicating greater metabolic health risks in older populations.
- State-wise disparities in healthcare charges suggest regional differences in healthcare delivery and costs.
- Although smoking is a known cancer risk factor, the dataset shows that a larger proportion of cancer-history patients are non-smokers, implying the presence of additional influencing factors.
- The analysis highlights the importance of preventive healthcare, weight management, diabetes monitoring, and early intervention programs to reduce long-term healthcare costs and improve patient outcomes.
